# 💰 Zero-Based Budgeting Guide

Your BudgetBuddy app now follows the **Goodbudget** zero-based budgeting methodology. Here's how to use it effectively:

## 🎯 Core Principles

### 1. Give Every Rupee a Job
Every bit of income must be assigned to an envelope. No "unplanned money" sitting idle.

**How to do it:**
- Go to the **Allocate** tab at the start of each month
- Distribute your entire income across envelopes
- The "Unallocated" amount should be ₹0

### 2. Use Envelopes (Category-Based Spending)
Money is divided into categories (envelopes):
- 🛒 **Needs**: Food, Rent, Utilities, Transport
- 🎉 **Wants**: Entertainment, Dining Out, Shopping
- 💰 **Savings**: Emergency Fund, Vacation, Goals

**How to do it:**
- Create envelopes in the **Envelopes** tab
- Assign each envelope to a category (Need/Want/Saving)
- Allocate money to each envelope monthly

### 3. Spend Only What's Available
You can only spend if the envelope has money remaining.

**How it works:**
- When adding an expense, you'll see warnings if:
  - ⚠️ No budget set for that envelope
  - ⚠️ Low balance remaining
  - 🚫 Over budget
- Dashboard shows "Remaining" amounts, not just spent

### 4. Adjust, Don't Break (Flexibility Rule)
Overspent in one envelope? Move money from another.

**How to do it:**
- Go to **Allocate** tab
- Click "🔄 Transfer Between Envelopes"
- Move money from one envelope to another
- Example: Food exceeded → move from Entertainment

### 5. Plan Before You Spend (Proactive Budgeting)
Budget at the start of the month, not after spending.

**Monthly workflow:**
1. Start of month: Go to **Allocate** tab
2. Enter your expected income
3. Distribute across all envelopes
4. Ensure unallocated = ₹0
5. Throughout month: Track spending
6. Adjust as needed using transfers

### 6. Focus on "Remaining", Not "Spent"
The UI shows how much you have LEFT to spend.

**Benefits:**
- Instant decision-making
- No mental math needed
- Clear spending limits

### 7. Budget for Real Life (Irregular Expenses)
Include annual/irregular expenses:
- Insurance premiums
- Festival expenses
- Car maintenance
- Medical emergencies

**How to do it:**
- Create envelopes for these
- Allocate monthly (annual cost ÷ 12)
- Build up the balance over time

### 8. Save with Purpose (Goal-Based Envelopes)
Savings are also envelopes with specific goals:
- Emergency Fund (6 months expenses)
- Vacation Fund
- New Gadget
- Down Payment

**How to do it:**
- Create saving envelopes
- Set monthly allocation
- Track progress on Dashboard

### 9. Shared Visibility (Accountability)
All family members can see the same budget.

**Benefits:**
- Better coordination
- Less financial conflict
- Shared responsibility

### 10. Build Habit, Not Just Track Data
Daily/weekly engagement is key.

**Daily habits:**
- Log expenses immediately
- Check envelope balances
- Review remaining amounts

**Weekly habits:**
- Review spending patterns
- Adjust allocations if needed
- Plan for upcoming expenses

- See unallocated money

### Envelopes Tab

## 🚀 Getting Started

### Step 1: Create Your Envelopes
Go to **Envelopes** tab and create envelopes for:
- All regular expenses (rent, food, utilities)
- Irregular expenses (insurance, festivals)
- Savings goals (emergency, vacation)

### Step 2: Set Up Payment Methods
Add your:
- Bank accounts
- Credit cards
- Cash
- Digital wallets

### Step 3: Allocate This Month's Income
1. Go to **Allocate** tab
2. Select current month
3. Distribute your income across envelopes
4. Ensure unallocated = ₹0

### Step 4: Start Logging Expenses
- Use the + button on Dashboard
- Select envelope and payment method
- Watch for spending warnings
- Check remaining balance before spending

### Step 5: Review and Adjust
- Check Dashboard daily
- Transfer money between envelopes as needed
- Plan next month before it starts

## 💡 Pro Tips

1. **Start of Month Ritual**: Allocate all income on the 1st
2. **Weekly Review**: Check progress every Sunday
3. **Emergency Buffer**: Keep 10% unallocated for surprises
4. **Realistic Budgets**: Base on actual spending, not wishes
5. **Celebrate Wins**: When you stay within budget, reward yourself
6. **Learn from Overspending**: Adjust next month's allocation
7. **Use Categories**: Group envelopes by Need/Want/Saving
8. **Set Goals**: Make savings envelopes specific and motivating

## 🎓 Common Scenarios

### Scenario 1: Overspent on Food
1. Go to **Allocate** tab
2. Click "Transfer Between Envelopes"
3. Move ₹500 from Entertainment to Food
4. Continue spending from Food envelope

### Scenario 2: Unexpected Expense
1. Check which envelope has extra money
2. Transfer to the needed envelope
3. Or reduce spending in other areas
4. Update next month's allocation

### Scenario 3: Got a Bonus
1. Add as Income transaction
2. Go to **Allocate** tab
3. Distribute bonus across envelopes
4. Prioritize savings and debt payoff

### Scenario 4: Month-End Surplus
1. Leave it in the envelope
2. It rolls over to next month
3. Or transfer to savings envelope
4. Build buffer for future months

## 📊 Success Metrics

You're doing well if:
- ✅ Unallocated money = ₹0 each month
- ✅ No surprise expenses
- ✅ Staying within envelope limits
- ✅ Savings envelopes growing
- ✅ Less financial stress
- ✅ Clear spending decisions

## 🆘 Troubleshooting

**Problem**: Always over budget
**Solution**: Increase allocation or reduce spending

**Problem**: Too many envelopes
**Solution**: Combine similar categories

**Problem**: Forgot to log expenses
**Solution**: Set daily reminder, log immediately

**Problem**: Income varies monthly
**Solution**: Budget based on lowest month, save surplus

**Problem**: Partner not on board
**Solution**: Share Dashboard, discuss goals together

---

Remember: The goal is behavior change, not perfect tracking. Be flexible, learn from mistakes, and adjust as you go!
